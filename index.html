Welcome

You are here because something is running without your permission.

A behaviour. A loop. A pattern you’ve tried to stop—but it continues.

This is not a discipline problem.
This is a loop control problem.

The INTERRUPT Protocol is designed to do one thing:

Break the loop in real time.

Not later. Not after reflection.
During the moment it happens.

What This Protocol Does

Every loop follows the same structure:

  • A trigger appears
  • A behaviour executes
  • A short-term relief is delivered
  • A long-term cost accumulates

Most systems try to change the outcome.

This system targets the only point that matters:

The moment between trigger and behaviour

That is where control is either lost or installed.

How To Use This Protocol

This is not something you “complete.”

This is something you run while the loop is active.

STEP 1 — Capture The Loop

When the behaviour happens (or just after), document it.

You will identify:

  • What triggered it
  • What you did
  • What it gave you
  • What it cost you

Do not analyse.
Capture reality as it is.

STEP 2 — Deconstruct The Pattern

You will map your loop into a simple structure:

Trigger → Behaviour → Cost

This removes ambiguity.

You are no longer saying:
“I procrastinate”

You are saying:
“After X, I do Y, which costs Z”

That is a controllable system.

STEP 3 — Install A Replacement Action

You will not remove the loop.

You will replace the behaviour with a smaller, faster action.

Your new action must:

  • Take less than 30 seconds
  • Require no motivation
  • Be physically executable

This is critical.

If the action is too big, you will fail.

STEP 4 — Execute The Interrupt

When the trigger appears:

You have 3 seconds.

Within that window, you must execute the new action.

No negotiation.
No delay.

If you hesitate, the loop resumes control.

STEP 5 — Track What Actually Happens

You will log:

  • When the loop appeared
  • Whether you interrupted it
  • What action you used
  • What the result was

This is not for reflection.

This is for pattern visibility and correction.

What Success Looks Like

Success is not:

  • Feeling motivated
  • Understanding yourself better
  • Wanting to change

Success is:

Control is behavioural, not emotional.

Important Constraints

  • Do not wait until you “feel ready”
  • Do not redesign the protocol mid-use
  • Do not choose complex replacement actions

If it doesn’t work, simplify the action—not your intention

When To Use INTERRUPT

Use this protocol when you notice:

  • “I can’t stop scrolling”
  • “I keep avoiding this”
  • “I know I shouldn’t, but I do it anyway”
  • Repetitive behaviours with immediate relief and delayed cost

Final Instruction

You are not trying to become a different person.

You are installing a different response.

Run the protocol on the next loop—not the next plan.
